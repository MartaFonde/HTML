The why of things

Why do the glasses allow us to see better?

The glasses are an invention of 1286. But they have been carving lenses from
practically the fifth century BC. The ancients knew that they allowed us to see enlarged
or better focused objects, but not exactly why.

Light is a set of electromagnetic waves that move around straight ray lines.

In the lens of the eye, those rays that come from an object change their direction, to arrive coordinately at the retina, where they
generate electrical currents that the brain interprets as images of objects.

In many people, the lens does not get electrical currents generated in a small number of retina cells, and the image is thick, blurred.

The glasses of the glasses change the speed of the light and, with it, the directions of the rays that reach the lens, so that the images of
new rays that come out of it now are clear.
